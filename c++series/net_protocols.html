Communication Protocols used in Robotics and Self Driving Vehicle Industry

Communication or Network control protocols are a set of rules which define how packets of information are exchanged between two or more systems via variations in physical quantities. These rules/protocols define the syntax, synchronization and the error recovery methods. A protocol can be defined using a software, a hardware or both.
The most basic elements of a communication system are the Server(Client), the Client(Server), the protocols, the data and the communication medium.
Some of the common Communication protocols are:
1. TCP (Transmission Control Protocol)
2. UDP (User Datagram Protocol)
3. CAN (Controlled Area Network)

Transmission Control Protocol
Let us take an example:
"Breaking news" a man is lost in the forest!!! Forest officers decide to send its two robots the airy(an aerial robot) and the landy(a ground robot). Landy uses camera to scan its area but it has a weak communication system. Airy also uses a camera with a strong communication system. Airy communicates with the base station. Both Airy and Landy communicate using a TCP/IP protocol (basically a WIFI connection). Landy finds the person and wants to send the data to Airy. Here Landy is the client and Airy is the Server.
